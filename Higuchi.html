Kyosuke Higuchi

Kyosuke Higuchi is a man driven almost entirely by ambition and self-interest. Unlike the more ideological Kiras,
he shows no concern for “justice”—only for personal gain. His arrogance is overt, and he carries himself with the entitlement of someone accustomed to power
and unaccustomed to consequences. Higuchi is impulsive, reckless, and easily agitated.
He lacks emotional restraint and often reveals his intentions through his reactions, making him more transparent than he believes himself to be.
Though he is competent in a corporate environment, his decision-making is shortsighted and fueled by ego rather than strategy.
His willingness to use lethal power for convenience and career advancement indicates a profound moral vacancy. This, combined with his impatience and
susceptibility to pressure, makes him dangerous—but also easier to corner compared to more calculated Kira suspects.
In essence, Higuchi is greedy, impulsive, and self-serving: a man who would the Death Note not as a responsibility,
but as the ultimate tool for personal advancement.
